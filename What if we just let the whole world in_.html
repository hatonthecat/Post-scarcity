In a time of national mourning (for precisely 70 million voters, + 10 million who might have stayed home), people wondering how (R) won the election this Tuesday have been longing for answers. While many have been pointing more fingers than Europe in 1914, the truth is that it is a combination of all of those things, plus one tiny little wrinkle thats been overlooked. Surely there are countless alternate realities that could be envisioned for Americas future from a Tarantinos Inglorious Bastards in 1944 to The Man in the High Castle to For All Mankind.

But for those who arent morally bankrupt, they may just be lacking in sci-fi ideas. I wont attempt to profess my idea is any better than others, but the world is looking a lot more The Expanse. Immigrants and the working class are the Belters. On the one hand you have a space force intent on colonizing Mars in an antagonistic view towards multiculturalism, and on the other you have a world that attempts to have at least some notion of pluralism and democracy.

Beginning in 2015, The Logan Act faced its first test of enforcement. Major deals took place (not unlike 1980) before the election, and frequent use of secure phones were used in the Russian and likely Turkish embassies in NYC by Americans (gee, I wonder why).

Go Forth and Diplomify

So now the country that once believed in a melting pot has a couple of choices. They can concede to this geopolitical tilt towards in North Atlantic sans NATO- the Russo-MAGA bromance, or, they can attempt to repeal the Logan Act because it has seen a double standard for at least 45 years. Unless you happen to find yourself in the good graces of someone because youre a basketball star like Dennis Rodman, its unlikely the average tourist is going to be able to exercise unilateral diplomacy. However, it shouldnt stop you from trying: Its Rick Steves advice. Ive always wondered whether the PBS channel was some front for American diplomacy, or some advertisement for consumerism. Its a little bit of both, but hear him out:

We are all Reginald Johnston today.

We are also all New Yorkers today. Because on November 6th, a majority of Americans voted for basically a blanket immigrant exclusion act, (unless of course, you meet the requirements of some arbitrary patronage to the GOP). While explicitly Project 2025 claims to want a skills-based immigration system like Scandinavia, historically unskilled labor built the metropolises of NY and Chicago. And the railroads.

Its one thing if there are no jobs because the party cant or doesnt want to create them. Its another thing to limit immigration on ideological grounds because the former seems to cause employment scarcity. The 1933-5 Civil Works Administration addressed this issue for every last American. The Works Progress Administration had the larger funding, but didnt give everyone a job.

Whats more important? Property values or Housing for All?

The crux the inertial matter/stalemate is the diametrically opposed values of property rights and and a right to housing. By preventing zoning reform, property owners exercise a form of market manipulation/control by keeping prices artificially high via regional scarcity (most people work in areas where jobs are available, such as cities rather than rural).

Theoretically, if the U.S. could allow as many people in as possible (upwards of 7-billion), obviously parts of the world would become far less populous than the U.S. This hypothetical scenario might sound like an exercise in reductio ad absurdum. But before a hypothesis can be made, it is first important to examine why exactly a population influx of say 7 billion is absurd. For example, doubling the population by 340 million alone would be quite significant. Some might feel that the U.S. is already crowded (Apu would disagree):

20x that might sound unsustainable. It probably is. But before I suggest something like 2 billion, I think its important to speculate exactly whether the U.S. could even support most of Central and South America immigrating.

The population of Central America is 184 million.

The population of South America is 440 million.

Even if every person in S. and Central America entered the U.S. legally, the population of the U.S. would still be less than 1 billion.

The square area of China and the U.S. is nearly equal, with around 3.7 million square miles. Chinas population is 1.4 billion.

Every day flights arrive to Central and Southern American airports, and theoretically more from other parts of the world could arrive somewhere south of the border and later cross into Texas, Arizona, etc. But the population would still not reach 1.4 billion anytime soon.

In the 40 years Ive lived in Illinois, the suburbs of Chicago have seen few homeless people in public. Occasionally, there would be some homeless people near the intersections by the I-290 exit ramps. This had always been the case. However, starting around 2017-2018, I would see immigrants selling water bottles and other things near the side of the road in suburbs, likely without a food truck nor permit like in Maywood on Roosevelt Road. I thought this was clearly an odd sight- as there had to be some useful work migrants could do besides standing outside. But of course, the immigration policy is nothing like that in the early 1900s. If one can make the argument that the country is full because zoning laws prohibit extra development, the opposite argument can be made: the country is not full and there is no need for zoning laws that limit development. One alternative to metropolises is developing larger medium sized cities like Kansas City or Omaha. But that too would be dependent on the willingness for cities to develop more private and public land. Since the new administration already has plans to use more federal land for oil drilling permits, I dont see a contradiction in the use of land for development. Its clear that one party values energy use/consumption/exports more than providing housing. In fact, both parties havent created nearly any affordable housing.

An Excerpt from 1984

Since about that time, war had been literally continuous, though strictly speaking it had not always been the same war. For several months during his childhood there had been confused street fighting in London itself, some of which he remembered vividly. But to trace out the history of the whole period, to say who was fighting whom at any given moment, would have been utterly impossible, since no written record, and no spoken word, ever made mention of any other alignment than the existing one. At this moment, for example, in 1984 (if it was 1984), Oceania was at war with Eurasia and in alliance with Eastasia. In no public or private utterance

was it ever admitted that the three powers had at any time been grouped along different lines. Actually, as Winston well knew, it was only four years since Oceania had been at war with Eastasia and in alliance with Eurasia. But that was merely a piece of furtive knowledge which he happened to possess because his memory was not satisfactorily under control. Officially the change of partners had never happened. Oceania was at war with Eurasia: therefore Oceania had always been at war with Eurasia. The enemy of the moment always represented absolute evil, and it followed that any past or future agreement with him was impossible.

The frightening thing, he reflected for the ten thousandth time as he forced his shoulders painfully backward (with hands on hips, they were gyrating their bodies from the waist, an exercise that was supposed to be good for the back muscles) -- the frightening thing was that it might all be true. If the Party could thrust its hand into the past and say of this or that event, it never happened -- that, surely, was more terrifying than mere torture and death?

The Party said that Oceania had never been in alliance with Eurasia. He, Winston Smith, knew that Oceania had been in alliance with Eurasia as short a time as four years ago. But where did that knowledge exist? Only in his own consciousness, which in any case must soon be annihilated. And if all others accepted the lie which the Party imposed -if all records told the same tale -- then the lie passed into history and became truth. 'Who controls the past,' ran the Party slogan, 'controls the future: who controls the present controls the past.' And yet the past, though of its nature alterable, never had been altered. Whatever was true now was true from everlasting to everlasting. It was quite simple. All that was needed was an unending series of victories over your own memory. 'Reality control', they called it: in Newspeak, 'doublethink'

'Stand easy!' barked the instructress, a little more genially.

Winston sank his arms to his sides and slowly refilled his lungs with air. His mind slid away into the labyrinthine world of doublethink. To know and not to know, to be conscious of complete truthfulness while telling carefully constructed lies, to hold simultaneously two opinions which cancelled out, knowing them to be contradictory and believing in both of them, to use logic against logic, to repudiate morality while laying claim to it, to believe that democracy was impossible and that the Party was the guardian of democracy, to forget whatever it was necessary to forget, then to draw it back into memory again at the moment when it was needed, and then promptly to forget it again: and above all, to apply the same process to the process itself. That was the ultimate subtlety: consciously to induce unconsciousness, and then, once again, to become unconscious of the act of hypnosis you had just performed. Even to understand the word 'doublethink' involved the use of doublethink.

China, if youre listening

About that Russo-Sino split in 1961:

To Mao, the events of the 19581959 period indicated that Khrushchev was politically untrustworthy as an orthodox Marxist.[38] In 1959, First Secretary Khrushchev met with US President Dwight Eisenhower to decrease US-Soviet geopolitical tensions. To that end, the USSR: (i) reneged an agreement for technical aid to develop Project 596, and (ii) sided with India in the Sino-Indian War. Each US-Soviet collaboration offended Mao and he perceived Khrushchev as an opportunist who had become too tolerant of the West. The CCP said that the CPSU concentrated too much on "SovietUS co-operation for the domination of the world", with geopolitical actions that contradicted MarxismLeninism.[39]

The final face-to-face meeting between Mao and Khruschev took place on 2 October 1959, when Khrushchev visited Beijing to mark the 10th anniversary of the Chinese Revolution. By this point relations had deteriorated to the level where the Chinese were going out of their way to humiliate the Soviet leader - for example, there was no honour guard to greet him, no Chinese leader gave a speech, and when Khrushchev insisted on giving a speech of his own, no microphone was provided. The speech in question would turn out to contain praise of the US President Eisenhower, whom Khrushchev had recently met, obviously an intentional insult to Communist China. The leaders of the two Socialist states would not meet again for the next 30 years.[40]

Echos of this split are being heard with new ripples as North Korea sends troops to Russia as China sits idly:

North Korea is also a buffer from South Korea. A less defended border, no matter how fewer personnel, can pose a new concern for China, however unlikely.

has become an uber-gentrified borough for the super-rich, and nothing like the the idea of an affordable neighborhood with a bustling Canal Street could symbolize a rebirth of the American melting pot spirit and a new Democratic Party better than Little Italy and Chinatown just north of Wall St. Racial identities are not being emphasized here to symbolize exclusion of other races, but to continue in the spirit that Carmine DeSapio left off:

DeSapio's Italian heritage signaled the end of Tammany's longtime dominance by Irish-American politicians, and he became the first nationally prominent Italian-American political leader.[3][4]
